Quantum Wealth Engine
The Quantum Wealth Engine is an advanced capital accumulation and financial independence (FIRE) simulator designed to operate in global contexts with dynamic fiscal and macroeconomic vectors.

🚀 About the Project
This engine allows you to project the evolution of an investment portfolio over decades, integrating variables such as salary progression, dividend yields, market volatility, and the corrosive impact of inflation on real purchasing power. The goal is to provide a mathematically precise view of the viability of achieving financial independence.

🛠️ Technologies Used
HTML5 / CSS3: Responsive structure and styling.

Tailwind CSS: Design framework for a modern, "dark mode" first interface.

Chart.js: Data visualization library for rendering financial time-series graphs.

JavaScript (Vanilla): Computational logic for the financial engine (compound interest calculations, currency deflation, and market cycles).

⚙️ Key Features
Worldwide Fiscal Engine: Define tax rates specific to your country of residence.

Dividend Strategy: Toggle between automatic reinvestment (Accumulating ETF style) or manual cash flow management.

Real vs. Nominal: Integrated inflation engine that converts nominal values into real-world purchasing power.

FIRE Projection: Dynamic milestone indicator showing the exact year of financial independence based on real dividends.

Market Regimes: Simulation of stable or volatile market scenarios (with cyclical shocks).

📊 Engine Structure
The financial calculation is performed through a high-precision iterative loop. If the inflation engine is active, the simulation extends up to 1,000 years to find the exact crossover point where real income meets your established target.

Technical Implementation Notes
FIRE Coupling: The milestone calculation is strictly coupled to the inflation engine to prevent distorted perceptions of wealth.

Cash Flow Security: The system accounts for capital "leakage" if you choose not to reinvest 100% of dividends, recalculating the compound interest curve in real-time.
